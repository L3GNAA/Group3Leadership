üí° Why This Matters

In real organizations, PMOs don't just manage individual projects‚Äîthey manage portfolios of projects. This assignment gives you hands-on practice with that critical responsibility.

üë• Step 1: Check In with Your Classmates

Before creating the portfolio page, connect with your peers to understand the current state of all projects. This collaborative discussion helps identify patterns and prepares you for the synthesis work ahead.

üó£Ô∏è Discussion Topics

Share Where Your Project Stands

Each team member should briefly share:

  • Current status: What phase are you in? What's been completed?
  • What's going well: Successes, milestones achieved, things on track
  • Challenges or risks: Blockers, uncertainties, resource issues, scope concerns
  • Recent changes: Pivots, scope adjustments, timeline shifts
  • Next milestones: What's coming up in the near term?

Offer Perspectives and Insights

As classmates share their updates:

  • Ask clarifying questions: Help teammates articulate their status clearly
  • Spot issues or opportunities: A fresh perspective may identify risks or solutions
  • Share similar experiences: "We faced that too, here's what helped..."
  • Suggest resources: Tools, approaches, or contacts that might help
  • Validate concerns: Sometimes knowing others face similar challenges helps

Capture Patterns Across Projects

As a group, identify common themes:

  • Common blockers: Are multiple teams facing similar obstacles?
  • Recurring themes: What patterns emerge in challenges, successes, or approaches?
  • Shared progress trends: Are most projects ahead, on track, or behind schedule?
  • Resource constraints: What resource limitations appear across the portfolio?
  • Risk correlations: Do certain risks appear in multiple projects?

üí° Facilitation Tips

  • Keep it concise: Set a 2-3 minute limit per project to stay on schedule
  • Take notes: Capture key points that will inform the portfolio page
  • Use a round-robin format: Ensure everyone gets equal voice time
  • Focus on current state: Don't rehash old history‚Äîwhat's the status now?
  • Be supportive: Create a safe space to share challenges honestly

‚úÖ Step 1 Checklist

  • Schedule and conduct a team check-in meeting (in person or virtual)
  • Each team member shares their project status (2-3 minutes each)
  • Group discusses common patterns, themes, and insights
  • Designate note-taker to capture key information for portfolio page
  • Identify overall portfolio health and common risks

üìÑ Step 2: Create a Shared "Portfolio View" Page

Collaborate to produce one collective page representing your class's overall project portfolio. This page should look and read as if a single, unified PMO team prepared it for executive stakeholders.

üéØ Key Requirements

1. Cohesive Tone and Formatting

  • Unified voice: Should read as one document, not patchwork of individual styles
  • Consistent formatting: Same structure, fonts, spacing, and visual treatment
  • Professional quality: Executive-ready presentation, free of errors
  • Design consistency: Use standard headings, colors, and layout elements

2. Avoid Copy/Paste from Individual Updates

  • Synthesize information: Don't just paste individual status reports
  • Rewrite in consistent voice: One person or small team should edit for consistency
  • Standardize language: Use same terminology and phrasing patterns
  • Eliminate redundancy: Combine similar information, remove duplicate points

üí° Collaboration Best Practices

  • Designate an editor: One person ensures consistent voice and formatting
  • Use shared document: Google Docs, Office 365, or similar collaborative platform
  • Set deadlines: Each person submits project info by X date, editing complete by Y date
  • Review together: Final group review before submission to catch inconsistencies
  • Keep it professional: Use business language, proper grammar, complete sentences

‚úÖ Step 2 Checklist

  • Each person provides raw project information using the template
  • Editor(s) rewrite entries in consistent, professional voice
  • Apply uniform formatting across all project entries
  • Ensure health bubbles, structure, and tone are consistent
  • Proofread for grammar, spelling, and professional quality

üìä Step 3: Include a Short Portfolio Overview

Begin your portfolio page with a 3‚Äì5 sentence executive summary that highlights overall trends and insights across all projects. This gives stakeholders the big picture at a glance.

üéØ What to Include in the Overview

Portfolio Health Metrics

How many projects are on track vs. at risk?

  • Count of projects by status: üü¢ On Track, üü° At Risk, üî¥ Off Track
  • Percentage distribution (e.g., "75% on track, 20% at risk, 5% off track")
  • Overall health trend (improving, stable, declining)

Common Challenges or Milestones

What patterns appear across the portfolio?

  • Recurring challenges (e.g., "Three projects face API integration complexity")
  • Common milestones (e.g., "Most teams entering testing phase this week")
  • Shared dependencies or blockers
  • Resource constraints affecting multiple projects

Overall Program Assessment

What is the overall health of the program?

  • General momentum and progress (strong, moderate, concerning)
  • Key risks to the overall program
  • Notable successes or wins across projects
  • Areas where the program is performing well vs. struggling
